Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program

Exploring America's Largest Food Aid Initiative.

By 2025, the United States faces intensifying economic polarization. Rising inflation, stagnating wages, and an increasing Engel coefficient indicate that a growing share of household budgets goes to food. With political debates around SNAP funding and eligibility changes under the current administration, understanding SNAP participation patterns is no longer just an academic exercise—it is essential for policy evaluation and civic awareness.

SNAP (Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program) served approximately 42 million Americans in FY2023, distributing over $113 billion in benefits. It is the largest federal nutrition assistance program and a critical indicator of poverty and social welfare. Yet most public dashboards present SNAP data only at the state level, obscuring the dramatic county-level variation that determines where resources are most—and least—adequate.

01 ABOUT

About SNAP

Showing SNAP data for:

Context

The Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program (SNAP) is the largest federal nutrition assistance program in the United States, providing benefits to millions of low-income individuals and families to help them afford food.

Audience

This dashboard is designed for policymakers, social researchers, and the general public interested in understanding SNAP participation trends and benefit distribution across American states in 2013.

Decision-Making

By visualizing SNAP data interactively, users can identify regional patterns, compare state-level participation rates, and explore correlations between demographics and program utilization to inform policy decisions.

01

USDA Food & Nutrition Service

Federal Administrator

The federal agency that directly administers SNAP at the national level. FNS oversees 16 federal nutrition assistance programs, serving approximately 1 in 4 Americans over the course of a year.

02

USDA Economic Research Service

Data & Research

The primary source for economic research on food and nutrition assistance programs. ERS maintains the SNAP Data System and Policy Database, providing data used by Congress and the research community.

03

Center on Budget and Policy Priorities

Research & Policy

A nonpartisan research institute promoting policies to strengthen SNAP accessibility. Their research shows every $1 in new SNAP benefits generates $1.50 in GDP during economic downturns.

04

Food Research & Action Center

Research & Advocacy

A leading nonprofit protecting and expanding federal nutrition programs for over five decades. FRAC monitors implementation of food assistance laws, provides training, and runs public information campaigns.

05

Feeding America

Food Bank Network

The nation's largest domestic hunger-relief organization with 200+ food banks and 60,000 partner agencies. For every meal the Feeding America network provides, SNAP provides nine.

06

Bread for the World

Policy Advocacy

A Christian advocacy organization urging decision makers to pursue a world without hunger. Their 300,000+ advocate network has been instrumental in protecting SNAP from proposed funding cuts.

07

MAZON

A Jewish Response to Hunger

A national advocacy organization fighting hunger among people of all faiths. MAZON invests in state-level advocacy and litigation to protect SNAP participants' rights, calling SNAP "our country's most effective defense against hunger."

08

No Kid Hungry

Share Our Strength

A campaign to end child hunger by connecting children to nutrition programs including SNAP. Research shows SNAP participation by pregnant mothers reduces the likelihood of low birth weight by 23%.

09

The Food Trust

Community Food Access

Working since 1992 to ensure access to affordable, nutritious food. Their Food Bucks program allows SNAP shoppers to stretch their benefits at farmers markets for fresh fruits and vegetables.

10

WhyHunger

Grassroots Advocacy

Co-founded by musician Harry Chapin, WhyHunger funds and connects grassroots organizations to advance the human right to nutritious food, working at the intersection of hunger, racial justice, and public health.

11

Hunger Free America

Direct Service & Advocacy

A nonpartisan nonprofit that assists low-income families in obtaining SNAP and WIC benefits while advocating for policies to end domestic hunger. Also connects families with private food resources nationwide.

12

National Association of Counties

County Administration

Represents county governments that administer SNAP in 10 states, covering 34.3% of total SNAP participants. NACo advocates for adequate federal funding and administrative support for county-level operations.

04 INSIGHTS

Key Findings

Finding

Regional Disparity

Southern states showed significantly higher SNAP participation rates compared to the national average, reflecting broader socioeconomic patterns and cost-of-living differences.

Finding

Urban vs. Rural

Urban areas demonstrated higher total enrollment numbers, while rural counties exhibited higher per-capita participation rates, suggesting distinct patterns of food insecurity.

Finding

Benefit Distribution

Average monthly benefits varied substantially by state, with differences reflecting local cost-of-living adjustments and household composition demographics.
